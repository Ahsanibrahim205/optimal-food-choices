Proteins

  • Proteins are the main component of all body tissue. They are:
    1. Made up of long chains of up to 22 amino acids (the ‘building blocks’ of protein), 9 of which are essential
    1. Needed for growth and repair
    1. An important component of enzymes, hormones, antibodies, etc.
    1. Used as a source of energy if carbohydrates and fats supplies are low
    1. Especially important for young, growing bodies.
  • Complete protein foods are those that contain all essential amino acids. These include: animal produce like meat, fish, eggs and dairy produce like cheese, milk and yoghourt.
  • Incomplete protein foods are missing, or do not have enough of, one or more of the essential amino acids.

    The body needs about 0.75 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight per day. Children, the very physically active, pregnant women and growing children need more. It is usually advised that protein foods make up around 15-20% of your diet.
